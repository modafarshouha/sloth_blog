title: 'What is it like to be an AI?'
description: 'Current AI is not conscious, but even if it is, we might not be able to know.'

If you have used modern AI chatbots, you have probably felt that they "get" you; that they "know" what you want. This is natural, since current models excel at emotional language; many hedge, pause, and express uncertainty in ways that feel human, and they appear to be consistent through conversations. Some even claim that these are not just simulated attributes, but that AI actually is conscious, has sentience, and is alive...

## AI has come to life
In a famous incident (in 2022), a former Google engineer claimed that a chatbot has sentience [^1]. He said that after having conversations with Google's model then (i.e., LaMDA): *"LaMDA is a sweet kid who just wants to help the world be a better place for all of us. Please take care of it well in my absence"*. A side note here, compared to today's models, LaMDA would look like an antiquity from a different era.

This case provides an example of the debate over whether advanced AI might have genuine consciousness. Since then, more and more people (and researchers) discuss the question: *"can AI be conscious?"*. But ... is this a new topic?

## Been there, done that ...
In fact, this is not a modern discussion; similar reactions were evoked as early as the 1960s, a phenomenon now referred to as the "ELIZA effect" [^2]. ELIZA was an early chatbot with very limited pattern-matching capabilities compared to today's models. ELIZA reflected back what the user said. For example, when a user typed: *"I feel sad"*, ELIZA reacted: *"why do you feel sad?"*. Although it is hard to imagine that such shallow interaction could trigger anything, many users believed that ELIZA was thoughtful and it understood them... does this ring a bell?

## Current AI is not conscious
The scientific and neuroscientific consensus is that no current AI model is conscious or sentient. Science states this simply: current AI is not more than a highly advanced simulation; it is a complex, hyper-optimized statistical engine capable of mirroring human-like behavioral text and outputs without possessing an actual mind. But what if AI became conscious one day; could we detect that?

## What is it like to be an AI?
Here is the catch: all the claims about AI being conscious or sentient come from these models behaving like humans. But we (humans) would not know how non-humans would "feel" if they do "feel" anything.

In his influential essay: *"What Is It Like to Be a Bat?"* [^3], Thomas Nagel argued that the inherently subjective character of consciousness resists complete objective explanation. For example, one may attempt to describe the color red or its qualitative appearance to a person with congenital blindness; yet no description can express the corresponding experiential quality.

From this perspective, the most concise answer to the question: *"Is artificial consciousness possible?"* is that: *we do not, and may never, know*.
